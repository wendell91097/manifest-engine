# MANIFEST ENGINE

*Narrative Framework — Design & Reference Document*

Post-Apocalyptic Setting: Lake Michigan Basin • Year 37 A.D. (After Destruction) • Version 15

---

## 1. The Central Theme

The Manifest Engine is built around a single animating question: what do imperfect beings do with imperfect interpretations of an imperfect world. Do they find true meaning within the condition they were born into, or do they reject meaning entirely.

This is not a game about war or politics or survival as ends in themselves. Those are the surface. Underneath is the human condition in its most exposed state — the bronze age collapse made literal. The old gods are dead. The institutions that organized the pre-war civilization — the state, the market, the social contract, scientific rationalism as public religion, the nation as sacred community — were the operating mythology of a world that believed in itself completely. The Destruction killed them in an afternoon. What remains are the competing claims to the inheritance of gods whose corpses are still warm enough to generate heat.

The factions that emerged from the ruins are not new religions replacing the old gods. They are the competing mythologies trying to become the new gods. Each takes something true about human experience, magnifies it into the organizing principle of an entire way of life, and defends it with everything available because without the principle the raw condition is what remains. Some will become the new gods. Most will not. The player lives in the window between the collapse and whatever the classical period will be — the window in which monotheism, philosophy, democracy, and tragedy were invented simultaneously the first time, because those are the things you invent when the old certainties are gone and you have to build meaning from scratch.

## 2. What Is — The World at Year 37 A.D.

Before any theory can be understood, the reality it describes must be. The setting is not backdrop. It is the argument everyone is having made physical.

### 2.1 The Basin at a Glance

The Lake Michigan basin in Year 37 A.D. is a region of approximately 150-mile radius centered on the greater Chicagoland area. Total current population: approximately 600,000. Before the Destruction the greater metropolitan area held nearly 15 million. The difference is thirty-seven years of starvation, cold, violence, disease, and the specific brutality of a world whose systems of mutual support collapsed simultaneously.

Eight major factions organize the surviving population in different configurations around different answers to the same questions. The majority of the population — roughly 500,000 — lives in independent settlements outside any faction's direct organizational control, navigating the competing pressures of the factions that surround them as a daily survival practice. These independent settlements are the basin's political mass. The factions compete for their loyalty, their labor, their food, and their children.

The Illinois River is the basin's main artery. Everything that matters moves on the water or fights over who controls it. At its eastern end the Chicago River mouth, controlled by the Luminaries. At its western end the Channahon confluence, controlled by the Algorithm. Between them: the trade route, the battlefield, and the clock. The clock is the circle of fire advancing at approximately two miles per year, currently fifteen miles in radius, planning the engineering that will eventually redirect the river itself to allow the fire to continue.

### 2.3 The Immediate Pressures

Three pressures are building simultaneously and will converge within five to ten years.

The fire advances. The Green Hands' burn perimeter is currently fifteen miles in radius. The Fox River has already been partially diverted to allow the fire to cross its banks. The Illinois River diversion is in planning. When it begins, every faction downstream loses water infrastructure simultaneously. The fire and the water crisis are a two-front event that only the Shepherd currently understands as coordinated, through satellite observation he has not shared.

The succession cracks. The Luminaries' First Keeper is dying. The Algorithm's Shepherd is mortal and his succession problem has no solution that preserves the mythology intact. The Watch's code has no provisions for peace or governance, both of which the Watch's success is producing. The Green Hands' Tender Shoots are approaching the collective refusal that will trigger civil war. Every major faction is simultaneously in internal transition.

The outside arrives. The Free Folk are already pushing beyond the basin's familiar edges. When they return with news of what else survived the Destruction — and they will — the basin's self-understanding as the remnant of civilization will shatter. The Shepherd already knows the basin is not alone. He has been deciding for thirty-seven years what to do with that knowledge.

### 2.4 The Geography

- **Northshore** (Evanston, Wilmette, Winnetka): Player landing zone. Shore settlements are the oldest surviving communities in the basin. They know something is in the lake. They have been deciding for thirty-seven years not to know more specifically.
- **Chicago Loop**: Luminary territory. Steel frame skyscrapers survived the airburst. Light hierarchy runs vertically. The elevated Loop — the rectangle of track that circles downtown — runs on the restored grid and is both the faction's most visible logistical advantage and the architectural expression of their theology.
- **O'Hare ruins**: Watch compound. Largest flat defensible ground in the region. Controls western road approaches and the basin's best maintained road network.
- **The Deadlands** (south and southwest, Midway area): Ground burst zone. Still radiologically active. Nothing lives there willingly. The most honest ground in the basin.
- **Channahon confluence**: Algorithm territory. Hardened pre-war data center. Fusion-powered. Satellite uplink capable. The Shepherd knows about the Illinois diversion and has not issued an output about it.
- **Lemont**: Merchants Union hub. The genuine neutral ground of the basin. The equilibrium point between the Algorithm's gravitational field and the Luminaries'.
- **Starved Rock**: Pax Mullebris primary encampment. Sandstone canyon network on the Illinois River. Canyon walls carry twenty-two years of glyphs. Directly in the path of the fire and the failing water.
- **Sugar Grove / Fox River valley**: Green Hands territory. Seed bank, underground greenhouse complex, Fox River diversion infrastructure.
- **Western prairie**: Independent farming communities between the Watch's territory and the approaching fire. The Free Folk move through here.
- **Lake Michigan**: Enormous, cold, incompletely mapped. The Bathyal diaspora is scattered across the lake.
- **Southern suburbs** (radiation band): Partial fission scatter zone. Elevated but survivable. The Repentant consider it honest territory. The Butchers operate in the southern portion.

### 2.5 Transportation

How the basin moves is an expression of its ideology as much as its logistics. Each faction's primary transport mode reflects the resources and values that define them.

**Water travel** is the basin's circulatory system and its most sophisticated mode outside Luminary territory. The Free Folk have had thirty-seven years to develop boat culture — sail and oar for most of it, reliable, fuel-independent, maintainable with available materials. Some biodiesel-burning river craft exist where factions can supply fuel, concentrated around Merchants Union trade routes and the Algorithm's toll infrastructure. The Luminaries' lock at the river mouth controls which boats move between the lake and the inland waterways — not just tolls but the physical ability to strand a fleet. The Free Folk and the Merchants Union have developed a practical counter: mooring vessels outside the chokepoints, ground transporting cargo to the other side, and relaunching on pre-moored or newly built boats. This maneuver is visible and irritating to both the Algorithm and the Luminaries, who regard it as an ideological provocation as much as a logistical one.

**The L** is the Luminaries' theology made mechanical. The elevated Loop — the rectangle of track circling the downtown core — runs on the restored electrical grid. Riding it is participation in the theology: elevation as hierarchy, light powering movement, the city organizing itself vertically around who ascends and who does not.

**Horses came back.** The grassland ecosystems of the western prairie recovered faster than most. By Year 37 there is a meaningful horse population concentrated in the independent prairie settlements and increasingly managed by the Watch. The Free Folk use horses for overland movement between river stretches.

**Bicycles are everywhere** in a way nobody has formally acknowledged as significant. Pre-war bikes still function with basic maintenance. In the independent settlements and among the Merchants Union's smaller traders, bicycles are the practical workhorse of short-to-medium distance movement — politically invisible because they are not a faction technology.

**Fuel and internal combustion** are effectively dead. By Year 37 every pre-war petroleum reserve is either exhausted or degraded beyond use. Conventional internal combustion survives only where a faction has stood up local biofuel production.

**The Algorithm's fusion power** is the wild card. Channahon has essentially unlimited electricity. Whether the Shepherd has directed it toward vehicle charging infrastructure or kept it focused on the data center and the toll systems is an open question.

## 3. How We Got Here

### 3.1 The Destruction — 2059

The Destruction was a global nuclear exchange, not a targeted strike on a single city. Chicago was one of many primary targets. Every major population center, military installation, and industrial complex in the northern hemisphere was struck within a window of approximately six hours. The specific Chicago detonations — an airburst over the Loop and a ground burst at Midway, with two additional warheads intercepted by the Channahon data center's automated missile defense relay — were part of a global event that killed approximately 2 to 4 billion people in the first year through blast, radiation, and the immediate collapse of every interdependent system that fed populations far beyond their local carrying capacity.

The pre-war technological state in 2059 meant the world had approximately 10 billion people, most of them dependent on global supply chains for food, medicine, and energy. Those chains collapsed simultaneously. The nuclear winter that followed failed global agriculture for three to five years. By Year 5 the global population was approximately 500 million to 1.5 billion, concentrated in the southern hemisphere, equatorial regions, and the specific northern pockets where local food production capacity and collective organization allowed survival.

### 3.2 The Nuclear Winter — Three Phases

**Acute Phase (Years 0–5)**

Global temperature drops 10 to 12 degrees Celsius in the northern hemisphere. The Great Lakes region, with its massive thermal reservoir, moderates the most extreme cold but loses reliable growing seasons entirely. The sky is wrong — a specific quality of filtered yellowish light from stratospheric particulates that everyone who survived it carries as a bodily memory. The basin's population of 15 million is reduced to approximately 800,000 survivors by Year 5.

This is the period that produces the Unmoored, the Butchers, and the conditions that force every surviving community into its founding decisions. The acute phase ran the threat system at maximum in everyone who survived it. The founding generation built their factions with the play system largely dark and the attachment system redirected toward collective survival. Every faction's foundational logic carries this mark — they solved the survival problem under conditions that would not allow for full humanity, and then institutionalized the solution.

**Transition Phase (Years 5–15)**

The stratospheric soot washes out unevenly. Temperatures begin recovering but remain 4 to 6 degrees below pre-Destruction baseline. The population continues its bleed — from 800,000 to approximately 350,000 by Year 15 — as food systems remain unstable. Factions crystallize not because conditions are improving but because organized survival is the only coherent response to sustained collapse.

**Stabilization Phase (Years 15–37)**

The acute nuclear winter is effectively over by Year 15. The sky is clear. Temperatures settle at 1 to 3 degrees below pre-Destruction baseline on average. The factions are fully crystallized by Year 20. From Year 15 onward the population begins a visible recovery — reaching 600,000 by Year 37 as restored agriculture, reduced territorial violence, and the first generation born entirely within the recovery reach maturity.

The first generation born after the acute phase is approximately twenty-two years old at Year 37. They have no memory of the wrong sky. They have no bodily experience of the threat system running at maximum during formation. Their play systems were allowed to develop. They are the first people in the basin who can hold the factions' foundational claims loosely enough to question them — which is either the beginning of renewal or the beginning of a different kind of collapse.

### 3.3 Flora and Fauna After Nuclear Winter

The ecosystem did not die. What collapsed first: insects, whose populations crashed when light and temperature disrupted reproduction cycles. Then the birds and small mammals that fed on insects. Then the larger predators. Then the domesticated animals that depended on human infrastructure. The agricultural ecosystem failed completely within two years.

What survived and adapted: the generalist species. Crows, rats, deer, raccoons, coyotes. Plants that tolerate cold, shade, and soil disruption. The specific mycorrhizal networks in the soil that survived because they live below the surface and process whatever falls from above. The aquatic ecosystems of Lake Michigan and the river systems, buffered by water's thermal mass.

The Pax Mullebris has been systematically mapping the transformed ecosystem. The specific plants that grew in the first years of recovery in contaminated soil are not the plants that grew there before. Some of what grows in the radiation band cannot be grown anywhere else. Some of it has properties that pre-Destruction botany never documented.

The Green Hands' fire is ecologically destructive in ways the Flame Council has not fully accounted for. Fire destroys the mycorrhizal networks that took decades to establish in the recovering soil. The Tender Shoots are beginning to understand this through the experimental crops outside containment, which are flourishing in ways the carefully managed interior crops do not.

### 3.4 Formation of the Factions — Formative Beliefs

Every faction is the radicalized survivor of a pre-Destruction ideological tradition that the Destruction either confirmed, corrupted, or forced to its logical conclusion.

- The Luminaries descend from the Enlightenment faith in progress — the deep cultural belief that human reason and technology are the mechanism of improvement.
- The Algorithm descends from 21st century technocratic rationalism — Effective Altruism, AI alignment discourse, the belief that human cognitive bias is the root cause of bad collective decisions. The Shepherd read the Destruction as confirmation. The tragedy is that his own irrationality — his specific vanity, his fear, his desire to be the intelligence behind the intelligence — is what corrupts the project.
- The Watch descends from the Stoic tradition running through Roman law, Kantian ethics, and military codes of conduct. The tragedy is that the code was written for war and the thing it is building toward is peace, which the code cannot survive.
- The Green Hands descend from deep ecology, seed sovereignty movements, and ecological grief. The tragedy is that purification logic applied consistently produces the eugenics allegory.
- The Repentant descend from the oldest religious tradition available — the understanding, running through every culture that has ever processed collective trauma, that suffering is meaningful and that the honest response to catastrophe is presence in the consequence.
- The Pax Mullebris descend from ecofeminism and the indigenous knowledge traditions that were systematically suppressed by the same civilization that produced the Destruction.
- The Free Folk descend from anarchist mutual aid theory, nomadic cultures, and the Norse tradition — the understanding that chosen obligation is more durable than imposed obligation. The tragedy is that chosen obligation cannot scale.
- The Merchants Union descends from Hanseatic League merchant republic theory and the libertarian contract tradition. The tragedy is that the market requires stability to function and stability requires something more than the market to produce.

## 4. The Factions

Each faction is a competing claim to the inheritance of the dead gods. Each contains something genuinely valuable and something that, followed far enough, produces a horror the founders would not have chosen. The factions are not good or evil. They are the full spectrum of human responses to catastrophe.

### 4.1 The Luminaries — Hope

The Keepers who understand the electrical infrastructure became a priesthood not through design but because knowledge that rare and that consequential naturally accumulates social authority. The lock at the river mouth controls the only navigable connection between Lake Michigan and the inland waterways. Light and water, the two resources the civilization most needs, both held by the same hierarchy.

The elevated Loop is the theology made mechanical. It runs on the restored grid. Riding it is participation in the doctrine. It is also the Luminaries' most visible logistical advantage.

The biological signature: the light hierarchy is a hormonal hierarchy. Who gets the light determines circadian rhythm, neurochemistry, seasonal mood, and long-term health. The Keepers at the top are genuinely different people, in a measurable biological sense, from the people at the bottom.

Internal fracture: the First Keeper is dying. Three candidates for succession are already in motion.

- **Candidate A** (male): Egalitarian redistributor. Organized the producer classes in protests that historically shut down production. Wants recognition for the people who maintain the grid over those who inherit it.
- **Candidate B** (male): Orbital visionary. Inspirational and divisive in equal measure. Certain the orbital beam will happen in his lifetime. The theology's natural endpoint.
- **Sera** (female): The succession's most dangerous candidate. An Algorithm plant — not by direction but by deeply internalized ideology. She wants the orbital link for the Algorithm, not the Luminaries. Her covert alignment is the most explosive political secret inside the Luminary structure.

### 4.2 The Algorithm — Reason

To everyone in the basin, the Algorithm is the actor that prevented the additional warheads and has been protecting the basin since before anyone knew it existed. The Shepherd — Gregory Ryan Jenson — is the name the man behind the hardened doors uses for himself in private. He is structurally absent from the mythology he created. He authors the Algorithm's outputs through terminals and synthesized voice.

The Shepherd has had satellite observation data for thirty-seven years. He knows about the Illinois diversion planning. He knows other survivor communities exist globally. He has been deciding, year by year, what to do with both pieces of knowledge. The moment the basin knows it is not alone, the Algorithm's mythology collapses.

Internal fracture:

- **Relay** (male): The only other person who knows about the Shepherd. Plans to preserve the illusion of the AI overlord when the Shepherd dies.
- **Victor**: An operator who has spent years noticing patterns in outputs, response times, and directives that reference events before data was logged. He believes in actually building an AI advanced enough to do what everyone already thinks the Algorithm does.

### 4.3 The Watch — Order

The Watch is not a mercenary organization selling violence. It is an investor taking calculated chances on people, settlements, and communities it believes are ready to be part of what the code is building. The code is not a product the Watch sells. It is a standard the Watch holds and invites others to meet.

Varek is the Titus archetype: pre-war soldier, incorruptible in the specific sense that he cannot be bought or threatened into betraying the code. He has never been made afraid. The code was written for war. It has no provisions for peace or governance, both of which the Watch's success is producing. He has been quietly aware of this for two years without telling anyone.

Internal fracture: the logistics officer (male), whose practical power has been quietly exceeding his formal authority; the two senior captains who disagree about what the Watch is for; and Cutter, the veteran who is the only person alive who can tell Varek he is wrong without consequences.

### 4.4 The Green Hands — Purity

The seed bank in the Fox River valley preserves pre-war agricultural genetics. The fire's history: Years 1–3 clearing at a quarter-mile radius. Years 4–8 the circle becomes legible at half a mile. Years 9–15 a mile per year. Years 16–37 two miles per year, current radius approximately fifteen miles, roughly 700 square miles of restored land.

The river diversion: fire needs dry ground. The Illinois River diversion is within the capability of their Automaton labor over five to ten years. When this diversion begins, every faction downstream faces two simultaneous crises.

Internal fracture: Flame Council versus Tender Shoots. Civil war trigger: a burn order the Tender Shoots collectively refuse. Contact has been made with the Pax Mullebris.

### 4.5 The Repentant — Guilt

The Witness carries a long rifle and walks barefoot. These are the same statement. The rifle is the extension of the seeing into the moment when honest navigation requires a specific act. The bare feet are biological regulation — warmth changes the body's state and changed states generate the overlay that occludes the raw condition.

The group numbers between twenty-five and sixty. Below twenty-five too small for the political complexity to develop naturally. Above sixty it starts becoming an institution.

The live theological question: if we are experiencing hell — if the wasteland is the hell the old world deserved — can we experience heaven? The Witness may have an answer. He does not share it easily.

Six figures emerge naturally within the group:

- **The Devoted**: the most complete expression of the theology in practice.
- **The Doubter**: does not understand how the Witness operates. Finds comfort in his presence nonetheless. This is its own kind of faith.
- **The Keeper of Pace**: practical, faithful, somewhat jealous of the Witness's stillness within the wasteland.
- **The Voice**: holds the redemption question most fully.
- **The Watcher**: the only person in the group with an accurate model of the Witness as a person rather than as a theological figure. The mechanical interface between the player and the Witness.
- **The Keeper of Breath**: the person upon whom the Witness depends more than has been acknowledged.

### 4.6 The Pax Mullebris — Transformation

The dead god they inherited: not a god but something older than any of the dead gods. The earth itself. The capacity of the earth to heal, to grow, to transform in response to catastrophe rather than despite it.

The Pax Mullebris is semi-nomadic. Starved Rock is the largest and most significant body in their system but the chain extends outward in every direction. The faction is a network of bodies in motion.

The glyphs: the canyon walls at Starved Rock carry twenty-two years of accumulated carvings that are not writing. The doctrine says nothing is written down — writing is fixed meaning, the pre-war civilization's method.

The Pax Mullebris is male-excluding by doctrine and founding necessity. Male emissaries are the faction's most deliberate instrument — men who completed a specific transformation process, tested against the standard of whether they can receive instruction from a woman with less external social standing and follow it without internal reservation.

Their military doctrine: poison, subterfuge, and the deliberate cultivation of internal division along gender lines inside enemy structures.

### 4.7 The Free Folk — Belonging

River traders and information carriers. Opportunists organized around the waterways. They trade, carry information, run cargo through or around the toll infrastructure, and raid when the opportunity presents itself and the risk is acceptable.

Leadership texture: chieftains are all men. The middle-aged lead as the balance between energy and wisdom. The elders are a respected knowledge source rather than an authority. This is not imposed hierarchy — it is the pattern that emerges from the specific demands of river life.

The collectivization potential: the right person speaking the language of chosen obligation, carrying enough story to be heard across groups, could temporarily unify them around a specific enough cause. The fire reaching the river is the most obvious candidate.

### 4.8 The Merchants Union — Exchange

The Merchants Union is not a band of traveling merchants. It is an institution. Twenty-two years of steady recovery have produced infrastructure — waypoints, carriers, storage facilities, settlement relationships maintained across seasons and leadership changes.

The share structure: voting power in Union decisions is proportional to share ownership. The internal fracture this generates — large individual shareholders consolidating voting weight against smaller merchants collectivizing shares to compete — is the pre-Destruction PAC dynamic made literal.

### 4.9 The Raiders

**The Unmoored**

Formed in the acute crisis years from a western suburb community. Substance from a pre-war pharmaceutical cache — MDMA-adjacent emotional dissolution, LSD-adjacent perceptual reorganization, opiate-adjacent analgesia. Originally medicine. Now dependency. The pharmacological hijacking of the seeking and reward systems simultaneously: not just pleasure-seeking but the collapse of the gap between seeking and finding. Everything becomes arrival. The horizon disappears. The oldest Shaman remembers what the ritual was for.

**The Butchers**

Formed in the radiation band during the acute crisis from a group facing starvation who applied livestock logic to a resource problem and discovered that fear generated by what they were doing was itself a resource. They are cannibals. Keeping women as breeders and killing men is not cruelty for its own sake — it is the livestock framework applied consistently to two different resource categories.

The Butcher camp survivor: the woman who has been there longest and understands the operation most completely has made contact with a Pax Mullebris emissary. Her goal has shifted from helping kidnapped women adjust to actively planning their liberation. This relationship is the most dangerous thing in the Butchers' operational environment and they have no idea it exists.

### 4.10 The Bathyal — NPC Population

The Bathyal diaspora is not a playable origin. It functions as an NPC population embedded in the shore settlements. Several Bathyal communities formed in Lake Michigan in the years after the Destruction. Those communities have since dissolved or scattered. Members who reached shore are not living openly as Bathyal — the tapetum lucidum makes that difficult in low light — but they are in the shore settlements, building the quiet solidarity of scattered people trying to find each other without drawing attention.

The origin question — genetic engineering versus radiation-accelerated adaptation in a 2059 world where that line was genuinely unclear — remains unresolvable from inside the body.

## 5. Trade and Exchange

The basin runs on barter. Thirty-seven years after the Destruction eliminated every monetary system simultaneously, no faction has successfully introduced a common medium of exchange that the whole basin accepts. What moves between people is goods and labor, negotiated directly.

### 5.1 What Actually Trades

Goods are the primary basis of exchange. The most liquid categories — the ones that trade in every settlement regardless of faction affiliation — are food, clean water, medicine, fuel, scrap metal, tools, and materials for construction and repair. Weapons trade freely in the independent settlements and among the Free Folk; less so inside faction perimeters where supply is internally managed. Finished goods (processed food, preserved medicine, fabricated tools) carry higher value than raw materials because they contain embedded labor.

Services divide into two tiers. Non-specialized labor — carrying, farming, construction, maintenance — is the basin's most common small transaction, typically denominated in meals or shelter by the day or week. Specialized labor — medical care, mechanical repair, navigation, translation, fighting ability, agricultural knowledge — commands significantly more, and the people who hold it are effectively mobile capital. A surgeon or a competent irrigation engineer is not a laborer; they are a resource that factions actively try to attract, retain, and occasionally coerce.

### 5.2 What Makes Barter Hard

Without a common unit of account, every transaction requires a double coincidence of wants — the person with surplus grain needs to find someone with surplus medicine who also wants grain. The Merchants Union exists primarily to solve this problem through network effects: they know who has what, who needs what, and how to route the exchange through intermediaries who make both sides possible. Their actual value is not the goods they carry but the information and trust infrastructure that makes the exchange happen at all.
Perishability creates urgency. Food spoils. Medicine has shelf life. A surplus today is not guaranteed tomorrow, which pushes settlements toward immediate exchange rather than storage and later trade. The Green Hands' seed bank and preserved food stocks give them structural power precisely because they have solved this problem at scale.

### 5.3 Where Faction Resources Fit

The factions do not operate outside the barter economy — they operate on top of it. Their strategic resources (the electrical grid, the river mouth lock, the data center, the seed bank, the Watch's fighting force, the Pax Mullebris' medical knowledge) are not currencies. They are coercive instruments: the capacity to make a settlement's situation better or worse regardless of what the settlement would prefer to trade.
The Luminaries do not sell light. They control who gets it, which reorganizes the neurochemistry and productivity of everyone inside their territory. The Algorithm does not sell information. It controls what information the basin treats as authoritative before any settlement decides how to respond to it. The Watch does not sell security as a commodity in a free market — it selects which communities are worth protecting based on whether those communities are ready to internalize the code.
The distinction matters: barter requires consent. Faction leverage mostly does not.

### 5.4 What Information Is Worth

Information about people — who owes what, who has cross-faction connections, who is not who their faction believes them to be — is the most dangerous tradeable asset in the basin, and the one that most closely resembles a currency in that it is fungible, portable, and does not spoil. The Free Folk carry it. The Merchants Union holds it. The Pax Mullebris has accumulated thirty-seven years of it from every woman who left a faction and arrived at Starved Rock.
The Shepherd has had a monopoly on the most consequential category of information — what the basin does not know about itself and what exists beyond it — for thirty-seven years. That monopoly is the Algorithm's actual structural position in the economy, and it has nothing to do with barter.

### 6.1 Established Cross-Faction Connections

- The Pax Mullebris network across every faction: Every woman who left any faction and arrived at Starved Rock carried her faction's internal knowledge with her.
- The logistics officer and the Merchants Union: His unofficial arrangements that keep the Watch solvent run through Lemont.
- The terminal operator and the Free Folk information carrier: Victor has been sitting with dangerous knowledge in isolation. A Free Folk carrier who passes through Channahon regularly has noticed.

- The Bathyal diaspora and the shore settlements: Some members of the dissolved Bathyal communities are in the shore settlements, recognizable to each other in low light through the tapetum lucidum.

- Varek and the local Free Folk chieftain: A relationship of mutual respect that neither party will fully acknowledge.

## 7. The Cosmological Framework

Everything observable in the world is an expression of a single underlying principle: vibration. Not as metaphor. As the actual operating logic of reality.

The fundamental quality of the universe is vibration. Everything vibrates. The differences between things are differences in frequency, amplitude, and the relationships between vibrating bodies. A piece of iron near a magnetic field doesn't choose to become magnetic. The field reorganizes the iron's internal structure through proximity alone. This is exactly what happens to people inside factions. The ideology doesn't persuade. It entrains.

### 7.1 Vibration as Biological Reality

The most obvious vibrations are the ones the body produces itself: brainwaves cycling through attention and rest and dream, heart rate accelerating under threat and slowing in safety, circadian rhythm organizing the entire biochemistry around the available light, hormonal cycles driving hunger and aggression and attachment and withdrawal.

Life is highs and lows. The heartbeat is a high and a low repeated until it stops. Every organism is a series of oscillations maintained within a range that sustains life. When the oscillations go too far in either direction and cannot return, that is death. When they are artificially flattened — as in the Unmoored — that is also a kind of death.

Every faction has a biological signature rooted in the substrate systems that define human emotional life. The Luminaries' light hierarchy is a hormonal hierarchy — literally reorganizing the neurochemistry of members through differential light exposure. The Flame Council controls the burn schedule and therefore controls the circadian rhythm of everyone whose life organizes around it. The Repentant's walking and fasting are deliberate disruptions of comfortable biological rhythm to maintain contact with the raw condition. The player moving between factions is undergoing repeated biological disruption, like severe repeated time zone shifts.

Understanding why this is true requires understanding the architecture of character itself — which begins not with beliefs or ideology but with the substrate layer of human emotional experience that precedes all of it.

### 7.2 The Stellar Map

The basin is a stellar map. The factions are heavenly bodies. Their positions, masses, gravitational fields, and the specific phenomena they produce generate the political texture of the world.

Channahon is a black hole. The Shepherd is the singularity at its center, emitting no detectable frequency of his own, present only as the source of the gravitational field that produces everything the surrounding accretion disk generates. The Chicago Loop is a bright star at the water's edge. Starved Rock is a denser body than it appears from outside — not luminous but massive. O'Hare is a brown dwarf. Sugar Grove is a young star in aggressive expansion. The radiation band is the nebula. The player is a rogue planet. The Free Folk are comets. The Repentant are pulsars.

### 7.3 The Phenomena

- **Gravity**: What vibration does when mass concentrates. Every Star generates it proportional to their accumulated mass.
- **Heat**: What vibration does when frequencies collide and transfer energy.
- **Light**: What vibration does when it reaches the frequencies a perceiver can register.
- **Darkness**: Not the absence of the universe. The presence of frequencies that cannot be perceived. The Shepherd is dark matter.
- **Balance**: Two vibrations in perfect interference, producing a stillness that is not empty but full. Lemont is the equilibrium point.
- **Resonance**: When two frequencies match, they amplify each other. This is the mechanism of faction cohesion and the player's most powerful political tool.
- **Dissonance**: When two frequencies are close but not matching, interference patterns are experienced as tension.
- **Magnetism**: Proximity reorganizes internal structure. Once magnetized a body generates its own field. This is how ideology propagates.
- **Event horizons**: The point beyond which escape from a relationship becomes impossible — not through force but through the geometry of the space the massive body has created.
- **The Ouroboros**: The snake eating its own tail. The basin at Year 37 A.D. is a specific moment in the Ouroboros.

### 7.4 The Witness as Fundamental Tone

Every faction is vibrating away from the raw condition. The Witness vibrates at the frequency of the raw condition itself. Not a different hallucination. Not a counter-frequency. The fundamental tone that everything else is a variation of.

The Witness does not exist within the passion system. His passion field is null. The macropassion score that governs every other Star's relationship with the player does not apply to him. This is not an implementation gap. It is the most precise mechanical expression of what the cosmological framework says about him — and what the character architecture explains at the substrate level.

### 7.5 The Player as Free Instrument

The player enters the world as an instrument whose tuning is not yet fixed. Moving between frequencies without being locked to any of them. The player who sheds every frequency they encounter and carries nothing forward is not free. They are empty. The factions vibrate because they believe in something. What the player believes — which frequency they eventually stop moving away from and start moving toward — is the game's deepest question.

## 8. The Generative Architecture

The Manifest Engine does not write characters. It configures the conditions from which characters inevitably emerge. A written character is a fixed narrative object — its depth is bounded by the author's imagination at the moment of writing. A configured character is a starting position in a generative system — its depth is bounded only by the grammar running forward from that position.

This architecture produces narrative possibilities at x^y scale, where x is the number of configurable dimensions per Star and y is the interaction space between Stars, between Stars and the player, and between Stars and world events.

The architecture has five layers, moving from biological substrate to crystallized drive. Each layer moves at a different speed. Each shapes and constrains the layers above it.

```
LAYER 0: SUBSTRATE
Universal. Biological. Pre-linguistic. Present at birth.
The emotional operating system of the species.
Cannot be removed. Can be suppressed, redirected, hijacked, starved.
Does not vary between characters — intensity profile varies.

        ↓ aimed by early experience and faction gravity

LAYER 1: DRIVES
The substrate systems developing orientation.
Where they point. What they have learned to want.
Slower to change than beliefs. Faster to change than axioms.
Faction gravity acts here during the formation window.

        ↓ rationalized and structured by

LAYER 2: AXIOMS
Foundational beliefs about the nature of reality.
What a character experiences as obviously true.
Not usually examined — experienced as the world itself.
Move on civilizational timescales.
The Destruction produced axiom-level shifts in everyone who survived it.

        ↓ generate and constrain

LAYER 3: BELIEFS
Specific propositions about the world.
Held, denied, or unresolved.
Updated by information through the three filters.
The working layer — where daily decisions are made.

        ↓ crystallize into

LAYER 4: PASSIONS
Beliefs that have crossed the activation threshold.
Future-directed drives emerging from belief states.
The surface the player interacts with.
```

### 8.1 Layer 0 — The Substrate

Nine primary emotional systems. Universal across the species. Each system produces a cluster of emotions — emotions are the system's activity becoming conscious. Each system has a dominant mode and a shadow mode produced by chronic suppression, hijacking, or starvation.

**S1 — Attachment**: The need to bond. To be known. To not be alone. Produces love, grief, loneliness, tenderness, trust, jealousy. Shadow: emotional numbness that reads as self-sufficiency; specific brittleness when proximity is offered unexpectedly. Faction gravity acts on this system by redirecting attachment from individuals toward the collective. Leaving a faction is grief. Betraying it is the specific guilt of severing a bond.

**S2 — Threat**: Pattern-detection oriented toward danger. Produces fear, vigilance, disgust, rage, anxiety, suspicion, relief. Shadow: permanent low-level anxiety that reads as personality; inability to experience safety even in safe environments. *Note on disgust*: disgust is the threat system applied to contamination and purity violation — which is why the Green Hands' theology maps so cleanly onto disgust psychology. The fire feels right before it is reasoned. Purity is the disgust system given ideological form.

**S3 — Seeking**: Forward-motion. Curiosity. The drive toward what does not yet exist. Produces curiosity, anticipation, wonder, enthusiasm, restlessness, boredom. Shadow when hijacked: the Unmoored have pharmacologically collapsed the gap between seeking and arriving. Nothing left to pursue. This system is the biological address of creative imagination — the organism's intrinsic drive to move toward what doesn't yet exist, generating the target in the process of moving. This is as close as the architecture gets to genuine generativity.

**S4 — Play**: Social improvisation. The suspension of ordinary rules. Produces joy (not satisfaction — aliveness), amusement, delight, playfulness, the specific pleasure of shared improvisation. Shadow: compulsive seriousness; humor that wounds rather than connects. *Critical*: the play system is the first substrate system to go offline under sustained threat. The acute phase suppressed it in everyone who survived the first five years. Every faction's founding logic has a rigidity that traces to this. The first generation born into stabilization — twenty-two years old at Year 37 — are the first people in the basin whose play systems were allowed to develop fully. They are the first who can hold the factions' foundational claims loosely enough to question them.

**S5 — Reward**: The pleasure/pain gradient. Approach and avoidance. Produces pleasure, desire, satisfaction, craving, disappointment, relief. *Note on hedonism*: hedonism as an axiom is the Layer 2 claim that this system should govern behavior. The system operates in everyone regardless of whether they hold that axiom.

**S6 — Status**: Hierarchical position tracking. Produces pride, shame, envy, contempt, admiration, embarrassment, honor. *Critical distinction*: shame is the status system — I am bad in others' eyes. Guilt is different — I did wrong regardless of whether anyone saw. Whether guilt is substrate or derived is the architecture's most consequential unresolved question. If guilt is substrate, objective morality has a biological grounding. If guilt is derived, the Repentant theology is the attachment system and meaning system running a story about themselves. The Witness walking alone through empty wasteland, guilt fully active with no social audience, is the game's empirical test case. This is not resolved. It is held as the live question it is.

**S7 — Care**: Directed toward dependents. Parental in origin. Produces protectiveness, tenderness, the specific grief of outliving someone in your care, the particular rage of the parent whose child is threatened. The Pax Mullebris' entire organizational structure is the care system redirected from biological offspring toward the earth's capacity to heal and toward women in danger.

**S8 — Lust**: Sexual desire. The drive toward physical union. The romance system in the engine is not primarily this system — it is the specific interaction between lust (S8), attachment (S1), and seeking (S3). All three must be active for what we recognize as romantic love. The population system connects here: children inherit substrate intensity profiles influenced by both parents' dominant system intensities — not drives, not axioms, not beliefs. Those are formed. Substrate intensities have a heritable component that precedes formation.

**S9 — Meaning**: Pattern-detection oriented toward significance. Produces awe, the specific distress of meaninglessness, reverence, the feeling of things mattering, transcendence, despair. This is what the factions are actually competing for — not the labor of the 500,000 independents, not their food or territory, but their meaning systems. The faction that captures the meaning system of a population has captured something the other systems cannot dislodge easily.

**Substrate intensity profiles** vary between characters — every system is present in everyone, but the relative intensity of each system shapes the character's emotional signature. The Shepherd's threat system runs at near-maximum while his attachment system is near-zero. The Witness's meaning system runs at maximum while his status system has dissolved to near-nothing. These profiles are the most stable layer of characterization — they predate and outlast every belief the character holds.

### 8.2 Layer 1 — The Drives

Drives are what the substrate systems become when they acquire orientation through early experience and faction gravity during the formation window. The same substrate system aimed differently produces categorically different people.

**D1 — Control Orientation**: S2 (Threat) primary. The need to manage the environment to prevent threat. High information-seeking, discomfort with uncertainty. Generates downstream: `knowledge.information_is_power`, `epist.reason_primary`, `onto.deterministic` (a deterministic universe can in principle be predicted and therefore managed). Rationalizes as: rationalism, governance, responsibility.

**D2 — Belonging Primary**: S1 (Attachment) primary. Identity constituted through connection to a group. Generates downstream: `human.chosen_obligation_durable`, `anthro.social_primary`, `eth.virtue_primary`.

**D3 — Autonomy Primary**: S3 (Seeking) primary. Identity constituted through independence from imposed structure. Generates downstream: `eth.subjective_morality`, `telos.created`, `self.ego` in some configurations.

**D4 — Mastery Drive**: S3 (Seeking) primary, S6 (Status) secondary. The need to develop and demonstrate competence. Generates downstream: `eth.duty_primary`, `epist.objective_truth`, `order.code_transcends_context`. Rationalizes as: excellence, craftsmanship, the code.

**D5 — Meaning Seeking**: S9 (Meaning) primary. The drive toward significance — the need for things to matter. Generates downstream: `telos.transcendent` or `telos.created` depending on other drive configurations, `cause.destruction_deserved`, `human.suffering_produces_growth`.

**D6 — Security Drive**: S2 (Threat) primary, S1 (Attachment) secondary. The need for stable conditions that allow prediction. The drive most activated by the acute phase and most expressed in the independent settlement structure. Generates downstream: `world.recovery_possible`, `order.chaos_self_defeating`.

**D7 — Novelty Drive**: S3 (Seeking) primary, S4 (Play) secondary. The need for new experience. Generates downstream: `onto.becoming`, `epist.experience_primary`, `telos.created`.

**D8 — Power Drive**: S6 (Status) primary. The need to be the organizing force in a situation. Present in all faction leaders — expressed differently depending on other drives. Varek: D8 + D4 (power as the application of mastery). The Shepherd: D8 + D1 (power as information monopoly). Generates downstream: `knowledge.information_is_power`, `eth.will_primary` in some configurations.

**D9 — Care Drive**: S7 (Care) primary. The need to protect and provision others. Generates downstream: `human.goodness_baseline`, `cause.debt_to_dead`, `eth.virtue_primary`. The Butcher camp survivor: her care drive is now aimed at the women around her, which is the most operationally significant redirection in the basin.

**D10 — Transcendence Drive**: S9 (Meaning) primary, S4 (Play) secondary. The need to connect to something beyond the self. Generates downstream: `telos.transcendent`, `onto.transcendent`, `epist.faith_valid`. The Witness: extreme — he did not find transcendence, he dissolved the self that was blocking it.

**Key insight on drive-to-axiom propagation**: Axioms are often the rationalization of drive configurations, not their cause. A character with D1 at high intensity arrives at `onto.deterministic` because a deterministic universe is a universe that can in principle be managed. The drive came first. The axiom makes the drive feel like discovered truth. The relationship is bidirectional and the specific direction of causation varies by character.

### 8.3 Layer 2 — The Axiom Network

Axioms are foundational beliefs about the nature of reality. A character does not typically know they hold axioms — they experience them as the way things obviously are. Axioms are organized in five domains. Within each domain, positions are often mutually exclusive — holding two incompatible positions requires suppression cost at the axiom level, which is the most expensive suppression in the architecture.

#### Domain I — Ontology: What is the fundamental nature of reality?

**`onto.deterministic`** — Total causality. No genuine free will. Everything that happens is the necessary product of prior causes. *Philosophers*: Spinoza, Laplace, hard materialist tradition. *Entails*: `epist.reason_primary`, `anthro.animal`, `eth.consequence_primary`. *Forecloses*: `self.culpability_personal` at zero cost; `eth.duty_primary`; `onto.transcendent`. *Note*: The Shepherd holds this at near-certainty while carving out himself as the one free rational agent. He has never noticed this contradiction. It is the most load-bearing incoherence in the basin.

**`onto.becoming`** — Flux is primary. Stability is a temporary pattern in ceaseless change. *Philosophers*: Heraclitus, Nietzsche, Deleuze, Buddhist impermanence. *Entails*: `telos.created`, `eth.subjective_morality`. *Forecloses*: `order.code_transcends_context`; `self.past_self_continuous`.

**`onto.being`** — Stable forms and essences are primary. Reality has real structure. *Philosophers*: Plato, Aristotle, natural law tradition, Kant's noumenal realm. *Entails*: `eth.objective_morality`, `meaning.discovered`. *Permits*: `self.purpose_legible`, `order.code_transcends_context`.

**`onto.transcendent`** — There is a reality beyond or above the physical. *Philosophers*: Plato, Neoplatonism, Kant, Kierkegaard, religious traditions generally. *Entails*: `telos.transcendent`, `eth.objective_morality`. *Forecloses*: `onto.material_only`, `onto.deterministic`.

**`onto.immanent`** — Everything is within the plane of existence. Nothing transcends. *Philosophers*: Spinoza (Deus sive Natura), Deleuze, Nietzsche, Stoic materialism. *Entails*: `telos.immanent`, `eth.subjective_morality`.

**`onto.material_only`** — Only physical matter and its processes exist. *Philosophers*: Democritus, Hobbes, Marx, eliminative materialism. *Entails*: `anthro.animal`, `onto.deterministic`. *Forecloses*: `onto.transcendent`, `anthro.divine`.

**`onto.consequence_total`** — The world as it is is the accurate and complete statement of everything that preceded it. Not determinism (mechanism) but consequence (disclosure). This is the Witness's own position — not derived from any philosophical tradition but arrived at through the dissolution of every framework that preceded it. It forecloses culpability while preserving guilt, which should be logically impossible. It isn't. The Witness is the proof.

#### Domain II — Epistemology: How do we know things?

**`epist.objective_truth`** — Truth exists independently of any observer. *Philosophers*: Plato, Aristotle, Descartes, Kant, scientific realism.

**`epist.perspectival`** — All knowledge is knowledge from somewhere. There is no view from nowhere. *Philosophers*: Nietzsche, Foucault, William James. *Forecloses*: `epist.objective_truth`, `eth.objective_morality`.

**`epist.reason_primary`** — Reason is the highest and most reliable faculty. *Philosophers*: Descartes, Kant, Hegel, Enlightenment generally. *Entails*: `human.bias_is_fatal`, `epist.objective_truth`.

**`epist.experience_primary`** — All real knowledge comes from direct experience. *Philosophers*: Hume, Locke, empiricism, phenomenology (Husserl, Merleau-Ponty). *Entails*: `knowledge.observation_sufficient`. The Witness's bare feet are epistemology — warmth changes the body's state and changed states generate overlay that occludes the raw condition.

**`epist.faith_valid`** — Faith and the leap beyond reason are legitimate paths to truth. *Philosophers*: Kierkegaard, Pascal, Augustine, mystical traditions. Reason has a ceiling. Beyond the ceiling is not nothing — it is the territory that only faith can enter.

#### Domain III — Ethics: What grounds moral claims?

**`eth.objective_morality`** — Moral facts exist independently of what anyone thinks or feels. *Philosophers*: Plato, Kant (categorical imperative), natural law tradition, G.E. Moore.

**`eth.subjective_morality`** — Morality is constructed, not discovered. Values are human creations. *Philosophers*: Nietzsche, existentialism, emotivism, Foucault.

**`eth.duty_primary`** — Right action follows from universal duty, not outcomes. *Philosophers*: Kant, Stoicism, deontological ethics generally. You do what is right because it is right. *Forecloses*: `onto.deterministic` at full confidence — duty without freedom is contradiction.

**`eth.consequence_primary`** — Right action is determined by outcomes. *Philosophers*: Bentham, Mill, Singer, consequentialism generally.

**`eth.will_primary`** — Morality is the expression of will. Power is the ground, not the violation. *Philosophers*: Nietzsche, Thrasymachus, Machiavelli. The Butchers are the most honest faction in the basin in one specific and terrible sense: they did not bother with the costume.

**`eth.virtue_primary`** — Right action flows from character. What matters is what kind of person you are. *Philosophers*: Aristotle, Confucius, Stoic ethics, MacIntyre. The Watch is reaching toward this without knowing it — the code is the scaffolding for character development, not the destination.

**`eth.hedonism_primary`** — The pleasure/pain gradient is the correct and sufficient guide to action. *Philosophers*: Epicurus (pleasure as tranquility), Bentham (pleasure as maximization). The system operates in everyone regardless of whether they hold the axiom.

#### Domain IV — Human Nature: What are humans?

**`anthro.animal`** — Humans are continuous with other animals. No special status. *Philosophers*: Darwin, Hume, Hobbes, Marx, Freud. Hunger is real. Fear is real. Everything else is what those drives look like when wearing the clothes of culture. The Destruction removed the clothes.

**`anthro.divine`** — Humans have special status — dignity, soul, or capacity that transcends animal life. *Philosophers*: Kant (humans as ends in themselves), Pico della Mirandola, Hegel.

**`anthro.rational_agent`** — Humans are primarily rational beings, capable of reason-governed action. *Philosophers*: Aristotle, Kant, Enlightenment, social contract theory.

**`anthro.social_primary`** — Humans are fundamentally social. The individual outside community is an abstraction. *Philosophers*: Aristotle (the political animal), Marx, communitarianism, Ubuntu philosophy.

**`anthro.will_driven`** — Humans are primarily driven by will, desire, and force — not reason. *Philosophers*: Schopenhauer (will-to-live), Nietzsche, Freud. Reason is the story we tell about what we were going to do anyway.

#### Domain V — Meaning and Teleology: Where does meaning come from?

**`telos.transcendent`** — Meaning comes from beyond the human. There is a direction given, not chosen. *Philosophers*: Plato, Aristotle's teleology, Aquinas, Hegel, Kierkegaard.

**`telos.created`** — Meaning is created by individuals through choice and commitment. *Philosophers*: Sartre (existence precedes essence), Camus, existentialism generally, Nietzsche (the Übermensch creates values).

**`telos.absent`** — There is no inherent meaning. The universe is indifferent. *Philosophers*: Nietzsche (God is dead — not triumphant atheism but mourning), Schopenhauer, Camus (the absurd). *Note*: The Witness does not hold this position. His `world.world_is_hell` is a moral category that implies judgment and therefore a standard. Absent meaning forecloses even that.

**`telos.immanent`** — Meaning is found within life itself. *Philosophers*: Spinoza, Deleuze, Nietzsche (amor fati), Stoicism. The Pax Mullebris hold something close to this — the earth's capacity to heal is not metaphor for something higher. It is the thing.

**`telos.through_suffering`** — Meaning is produced through fully inhabited suffering, not despite it. *Philosophers*: Kierkegaard, Dostoevsky, Viktor Frankl, Job. The Repentant theology is the most complete expression of this axiom. The Witness's walking and fasting are the methodology.

### 8.4 Layer 3 — The Belief System

Beliefs are specific propositions about the world held, denied, or left unresolved. They are what axioms look like when they encounter the specific conditions of a life. The same axiom produces different beliefs when filtered through different drives and different histories.

Beliefs are organized as concise keys across domains:

- `self.*` — ego, true_self_visible, past_self_continuous, past_self_dissolved, fear_accessible, redemption_available, culpability_personal, culpability_systemic, purpose_legible
- `human.*` — goodness_baseline, bias_is_fatal, contribution_determines_worth, chosen_obligation_durable, suffering_produces_growth, suffering_is_waste, order_is_sought, pattern_implies_purpose
- `order.*` — chaos_self_defeating, true_right_maintains_might, code_transcends_context, entropy_applies, governance_requires_code, peace_requires_more
- `cause.*` — destruction_preventable, destruction_deserved, guilt_structural, debt_to_dead, recovery_earnable, recovery_impossible
- `knowledge.*` — information_is_power, observation_sufficient, hidden_knowledge_obligates, rationalism_over_intuition, distributed_knowledge_safer
- `world.*` — recovery_possible, world_is_hell, basin_not_alone, civilization_worth_rebuilding, new_order_inevitable
- `faction.*` — assessment claims about specific factions
- `person.*` — assessment claims about specific named Stars; combinatorial, generated per meaningful relationship

**The three filters** govern how information moves through the belief system:

- **Filter 1 — Source Trust**: Who is delivering this and how much does this Star trust them. Trust is not faction-wide — it is relationship-specific and history-dependent.
- **Filter 2 — Belief System Configuration**: How does this information configure to the Star's current belief state. A Star whose belief architecture is internally coherent resists information that would destabilize it. A Star in active denial is specifically vulnerable to information that surfaces the suppressed claim from an unexpected direction.
- **Filter 3 — Community Perception Pressure**: What does believing this cost socially within the faction. Stars with developing cross-faction bonds absorb this cost more easily.

Belief states are stored as:

```
const starBeliefs = {
  starId: '',
  believes: [ { claim: '', confidence: 0 } ],
  unsure:   [ { claim: '', tension: 0 }    ],
  denies:   [ { claim: '', cost: 0 }       ],
}
```

Denial has a cost. A Star actively suppressing something their unsure layer keeps surfacing pays a price visible in their behavior — rigidity in certain value positions, uncharacteristic reactivity to specific topics. The cognitive dissonance lives in the gaps between categories.

**Knowledge arrives through typed sources:**

```
const KNOWLEDGE_SOURCES = {
  OBSERVED:    // sensory — witnessed directly, highest trust weight
  TRANSMITTED: // testimony — another Star or carrier told them
  DISCOVERED:  // empirical artifact — maps, records, physical evidence
  REMEMBERED:  // memory — prior experience, degrades and distorts over time
  REASONED:    // rationalism — self-reflection on existing beliefs
  INTUITED:    // intuition — unverifiable internal resolution
  INDUCED:     // player-caused realization
}
```

### 8.5 Layer 4 — Passions

Passions are beliefs that have crossed the activation threshold — accumulated weight and confirming evidence sufficient to become future-directed drives. They are not independent of beliefs. They are expressions of belief states. When beliefs shift, passion states shift accordingly.

Passions are the surface the player interacts with. They run from -100 to +100 through named threshold states: Devoted, Steadfast, Trusted, Favorable, Neutral, Strained, Opposed, Hostile, Irreconcilable. Macropassion is the aggregate. Fame amplifies ally relationships and dampens enemy relationships. Infamy inverts this.

**Passion states:**

```
const PASSION_STATES = {
  DORMANT:   'exists in pool, not currently active',
  ACTIVE:    'currently a dominant surface passion',
  SUBMERGED: 'was active, pushed below surface by belief shift',
  EMERGED:   'newly surfaced from dormant due to belief change',
  EXTINCT:   'conditions that generated this passion no longer exist',
}
```

The submerged state is the most dramatically significant. A passion pushed below the surface by a belief shift is not gone. It is waiting. When the conditions that originally surfaced it return, the passion re-emerges carrying the weight of everything that happened while it was submerged.

**The Witness and the null passion field**: Passions require temporal orientation — desire points toward a future state, fear points away from one. The Witness has dissolved temporal orientation. Beliefs accumulate in his system. They hit threshold. They do not convert — because conversion requires a toward and there is nowhere to go. His meaning system runs at near-maximum. The energy has nowhere to go except the present moment. The null passion field is not emptiness. It is fullness without direction.

### 8.6 The Claim Universe

The claim universe is the full pool of propositions any Star can hold, deny, or be unsure about. Claims are not assigned to characters — they float in the pool as raw material. A Star is partly defined by which claims their initialization draws on.

The claim universe spans thousands of propositions across the five belief domains. No Star holds all of them. Every Star holds enough to generate a coherent internal architecture. Each claim carries a polarity and a confidence weight. The same claim held at high confidence generates different behavior than the same claim held at low confidence. A claim in the denies category costs the Star something — denial is active suppression, not passive absence.

Person-specific and relational claims (`person.*`) are the most combinatorial portion of the claim universe — every Star has potential belief states about every other Star they have meaningful contact with.

### 8.7 The Interaction Grammar

The grammar is the engine. Everything else is initialization. The loop is:

```
// Information arrives from a source
// → filtered by source trust (Filter 1)
// → filtered by belief system configuration (Filter 2)
// → filtered by community perception pressure (Filter 3)
// → lands in believes / unsure / denies
// → belief shift triggers passion pool re-evaluation
// → passions surface (DORMANT → EMERGED → ACTIVE)
// → passions submerge (ACTIVE → SUBMERGED)
// → passions re-emerge (SUBMERGED → EMERGED → ACTIVE)
// → passions go extinct when conditions fully dissolve
// → sustained patterns over seasons move values
// → shifted values create new conditions that generate new information
```

### 8.8 Belief Visibility and Epistemic Access

Beliefs are initially hidden to the player. The player cannot inspect a Star's belief state through the interface. They can only infer it from behavior — and behavior is always a partial and potentially misleading signal.

Belief states are revealed through two paths. The first is genuine connection — sustained relationship at sufficient depth produces the specific trust that makes a Star willing to articulate what they hold true. This is not a dialogue unlock. It is a relationship state.

The second path is coercive access — blackmail, espionage, extortion, torture of proximate entities. These paths reveal belief states but damage or destroy the relationships that would otherwise allow the player to act on what they learn.

The player's knowledge of a belief state is always a snapshot, not a live feed. Beliefs shift. The gap between snapshot and current state is where the system's most interesting drama lives.

### 8.9 Initialization vs. Emergence

What you author as a designer is starting position and the conditions for movement. What emerges from the grammar running forward from that position is the character the player actually meets.

This means no two playthroughs produce the same Stars. The initialization is fixed. The emergence is not. The world state at Year 37 is the starting graph. The player's campaign is a traversal of that graph that permanently alters its topology.

Stars are not written. They are configured. The configuration is the authored object.

## 9. The Stars — Heavenly Bodies in Motion

Stars are not people who were appointed to significance. They are people who accumulated enough mass that other people began organizing their behavior around them without being told to. Each Star has a genuine reason to exist, a specific thing only they can do in the world, and a specific relationship to at least one other Star that generates orbital mechanics.

What follows are initialization files. The prose is annotation on the configuration — it describes the starting state, not the endpoint. The grammar running forward from these initializations produces what the player actually encounters.

### 9.1 The Two Value Systems

**Passions — Alignment With the Player**: Passions measure how well the player's actions have served what this specific Star cares about. Each Star has dominant passions drawn from a pool specific to that Star — not their faction.

**Independent Values — The Star's Internal Life**: Their position in the faction's internal power dynamics. Their ambitions. Their private knowledge. Their relationships with other Stars. They exist and move whether the player is engaged or not.

### 9.2 The Belief / Passion / Values Hierarchy

A Star is not a static object. They are a current state of something always in motion. At any given moment the player is seeing a snapshot of three layers moving at different speeds, each influencing the others. Beliefs are the foundation, updated by information. Passions are the expression of beliefs. Independent values are the slowest layer, moved by sustained conditions over time.

### 9.3 Initialization Format

Each Star initialization file includes five components:

1. **Substrate profile**: The relative intensity of each of the nine emotional systems
2. **Drive profile**: Which drives are dominant, at what intensity, and toward what target

## 10. Splintering — The Default Behavior of Living Things

Factions are not stable institutions with occasional catastrophic splits. They are the current visible configuration of constantly splintering and re-aggregating bodies. The fundamental unit is the splinter cell. Factions are the pattern that emerges when enough splinter cells share enough vibrational frequency to generate coherent collective biology together.

### 10.1 The Meso Layer — Subgroups

The unit of change is not the faction and not the individual Star. It is the subgroup. Subgroups form naturally within factions before any formal split occurs. They are proto-factions — collections of Stars and population with developing internal coherence and diverging interests from the parent body. Catastrophic fracture is the surface expression of subgroup formation that has been happening at the organic level for seasons before the event fires.

Design implication for Star counts: each faction needs enough Stars to represent its significant subgroups, not just its formal hierarchy.

### 10.2 Organic Splintering vs. Catastrophic Fracture

Organic splintering is continuous — tracked through the drift of independent values and passions across the Stars, below the threshold of formal events. Catastrophic fracture is when organic splintering reaches a threshold that reorganizes the faction itself. The player who is paying attention to the Stars can see catastrophic fracture coming before it arrives.

### 10.3 New Faction Crystallization

New factions form from subgroup matter when three conditions converge simultaneously in the same population body.

```
const crystallizationConditions = {
  conditionSaturation: 0,
  // How fully the shared condition has developed.
  gravitationalMass: 0,
  // Combined Star weight of the proto-faction's center.
  thresholdProximity: 0,
  // How close the triggering event is.
}
```

When all three cross their thresholds in the same window, the formation event fires. The system does not need to know in advance what the new faction will be. It knows the conditions. The specific character of what crystallizes is determined by which Stars are at its center and what beliefs they carry out of the parent body.

## 11. The INTERESTS Graph & Event Systems

The INTERESTS graph is the content-layer declaration of named stakes across and within Stars. It keeps content identifiers out of the engine. All evaluation goes through generic resolvers that know nothing about specific factions or passions.

### 11.1 INTERESTS Types

- **competing**: Both parties rising creates collision pressure. Convergence fires when they can no longer coexist.
- **aligned**: Both parties carrying weight creates opportunity.
- **internal**: Tension between two passions on the same Star.
- **rivalry**: The gap between an incumbent's relevant value and a challenger's practical power has crossed a threshold.

### 11.2 Event Types

- **Reactive events**: Fire when a single passion crosses a threshold in a specific direction.
- **Convergence events**: Fire when a condition across multiple Stars is met. Independent pressures collide. Cannot be avoided.
- **Splintering events**: Fire when rivalry trigger conditions are met or when organic value drift produces a threshold crossing.
- **World events**: Historical texture arriving regardless of player action.
- **Stranger encounters**: The guest pool system. Probability of encounter increases each season after the last encounter.

## 13. Where It Is All Heading — The Fifty-Year Spiral

The bronze age collapse already happened. That was Year 0. What follows is the classical period forming in real time. The basin is a crucible. The fire is the clock. The Illinois diversion, when it begins, changes the gravitational field of the entire basin simultaneously.

### 13.1 The Luminaries — Completion and the Orbital Gambit

The succession crisis resolves within ten years. The orbital gambit — complicated now by Sera's hidden allegiance — is not just a technological race but a political trap. Whether the directed energy serves the Luminaries or the Algorithm depends on who controls the interface.

### 13.2 The Algorithm — The Curtain Problem

The Shepherd is mortal. When the outputs stop, the contest between Relay and Victor determines whether the successor institution preserves the illusion or attempts to realize it. The Shepherd's knowledge of the Illinois diversion and of the global survivor communities will be part of what the terminal operators inherit.

### 13.3 The Watch — The Peace Problem

Graduated settlements no longer need the Watch. The frontier shifts to displaced prairie communities. The code has no provisions for governance. The Watch in fifty years is either the institutional foundation of a new political order or it has fractured into a revolutionary cadre.

### 13.4 The Green Hands — The Fire's Endpoint

The Tender Shoots' civil war happens within a decade. The synthesis that forms is the most consequential new political entity in the basin's next fifty years. The river diversion infrastructure outlasts the faction that built it — when civil war disrupts maintenance, the downstream effects become a second crisis that neither the Algorithm nor the Luminaries anticipated.

### 13.5 The Repentant — The Question That Answers Itself

The redemption schism resolves through the Witness's continued walking and what the basin's violence eventually produces in those who have been present for it completely. The Repentant's contribution to the classical period is the specific quality of witness that makes the basin unable to lie to itself indefinitely.

### 13.6 The Pax Mullebris — The Metabolization Completes

The fire reaches Starved Rock within twelve years. The canyon burns. The glyphs survive because the knowledge was never only in the canyon. The alliance with the Tender Shoots produces the first genuinely new agricultural methodology in the basin.

### 13.7 The Free Folk — The Stories Become History

The basin's political consolidation produces institutions large enough that the Free Folk's information economy is no longer structurally necessary. The Free Folk respond by going further. The stories they tell when they return will be the founding mythology of the classical period.

### 13.8 The Merchants Union — The Hub Becomes the System

The choice between remaining the equilibrium point and letting the system reorganize, or becoming the basin's first formal governance framework. If the second path is taken, the Merchants Union becomes the structure the Watch's governance experiments, the Tender Shoots' alliance, and the Algorithm's democratic contest all plug into.

### 13.9 The Raiders — Endpoints

The Unmoored disappear within one to two generations as the pre-war pharmaceutical caches are exhausted. Not through defeat — through the logic of their own dependency reaching its endpoint. The Butchers expand as their prey population collapses, pushing into Watch-contracted territory and Pax Mullebris operational radius. The internal division cultivated inside the camps for years fires from inside when the active phase begins.

### 13.10 The Synthesis

The classical period is not peace. It is the chaotic, generative, irreversible process by which the raw material of catastrophe is worked into something that will produce the next civilization. Not save the world. Shape what the world becomes. That is the only power available. It is enough.

## 14. Beyond the Basin — Spheres of Influence

The basin has been treating itself as the world. It is not. Thirty-seven years of parallel development in scattered survivor pockets has produced political configurations the basin cannot predict and does not know about. The Shepherd knows. He has been deciding for thirty-seven years what to do with that knowledge.

- **The Mississippi River Corridor**: An alternative gravitational center with different answers to the same questions.
- **The Great Lakes Network**: Lake Superior, Lake Huron, Lake Erie, and Lake Ontario have shorelines that supported pre-Destruction populations.
- **The Canadian Interior**: Canada was not a primary target. The northern territories represent a slow gravitational pull.
- **The Gulf Coast and Southern Regions**: The American South survived better than northern agriculture.
- **The Wider World**: The southern hemisphere survived the exchange with less direct damage. The Shepherd's satellite shows significant heat signatures indicating concentrated human activity in all of these regions. The world is not empty. The world is full. The basin just doesn't know it yet.

## 16. The Tutorial — The Catch

Over four hundred people in the old northshore lakefront park infrastructure. A body at the waterline, carrying a symbol the shore people recognize and won't explain.

The Saltmen: started as traders, found a business model in isolated settlements with no outside protection. They control the boat repair equipment and take a cut of every catch. ### A.4 The Passion-Value Distinction in Code

Passions are stored on each Star object under the passions key with value, lastChanged, and why fields. Modified by applyE() in response to player actions. Decay through the passion decay loop in the ADVANCE reducer. Each Star's passion pool is drawn from that Star's own pool of possible passions — not a faction-wide pool.

Independent values are stored under a separate values key. Modified by world event resolvers, splintering event checkers, and the rivalry trigger system. NOT modified by applyE() and NOT included in the standard passion decay loop.

### A.8 The Resolver System

- **resolveReactiveTrigger(trigger, stars, prevStars)**: Single-Star passion crossing checks with optional macropassion gates.
- **resolveCondition(trigger, stars, state)**: Dispatches to sub-resolvers for convergence events. Types: interest, macroAll, rivalry.

- **resolveMacroAll(trigger, stars, state)**: Iterates revealedStars, evaluates |macropassion| >= minAbsMacro for each.

### A.9 The INTERESTS Object

Content-layer declaration of named stakes. The engine resolver reads party entries and evaluates conditions generically — it never contains specific Star IDs. Types: competing, aligned, internal, rivalry. Queryable by UI components to display live tension without those components knowing which Stars are involved.

The machine runs when any Star's alive flag flips false, when a rivalry trigger fires, or when organic independent value drift crosses the organic splinter threshold.

### A.12 Axiom Propagation Rules

Axiom propagation is one-directional at initialization and bidirectional during gameplay. At initialization: drives generate axiom positions (bottom-up). During gameplay: axiom shifts cascade to belief availability (top-down), and sufficiently large belief shifts accumulated over sustained periods can generate drive drift which eventually produces axiom reconsideration.

Axiom shifts during gameplay are rare and significant. They require: sustained contradiction from high-trust sources across multiple filters, prolonged exposure to a frequency incompatible with the current axiom position, or a single witnessed event of sufficient weight to destabilize the foundational claim. The Destruction produced axiom-level shifts in everyone who survived it. Events of equivalent weight are the only in-game mechanism for equivalent shifts.

### A.13 The Population System and Generational Architecture

Children enter the system at Layer 0 only. Substrate intensities have a heritable component influenced by both parents' dominant system intensities. All other layers form through experience:

- **Layer 1 (Drives)**: Formation window approximately Years 0–12 of a character's life. Primary influence: faction environment (if inside faction territory) or independent settlement conditions (if outside). Secondary influence: primary attachment figures and their own drive profiles.
- **Layer 2 (Axioms)**: Formation window approximately Years 8–25. Primary influence: the claim universe of the formation environment. Secondary influence: witnessed events that confirm or destabilize early axiom positions.
- **Layer 3 (Beliefs)**: Continuous formation from first language onward.

The first generation born entirely post-Destruction (age 22 at Year 37) is the architecture's most significant cohort: substrate systems formed during stabilization rather than acute phase, drives formed inside faction gravity but without the founders' direct trauma, axioms formed without any experiential reference to the world that was destroyed. They are the basin's first people who hold the factions' foundational claims as received truths rather than hard-won conclusions. This makes them simultaneously the basin's most ideologically malleable population and its most capable of genuine synthesis.
